items:
  - q: "Is the consultation free?"
    a: "Yes, completely free. No charges, no hidden fees, no commitment. We believe good guidance shouldn't cost anything upfront."
  - q: "Is it really free to study in Germany?"
    a: "Public universities charge only a small semester fee, typically €150–€350. That's it. No tuition. It applies to international students from India too."
  - q: "What documents do I need for a Germany student visa?"
    a: "University admission letter, valid passport, blocked account (€11,208), health insurance, academic transcripts with certified translations, APS certificate and biometric photos. We prepare and review every document with you."
  - q: "What is a blocked account and how much do I need?"
    a: "A mandatory bank account proving you can fund your stay. You deposit €11,208 for the year and it's released to you monthly once you're in Germany. We guide you through Fintiba or Expatrio."
  - q: "Do I need to know German to study?"
    a: "No. Hundreds of master's programs are taught fully in English. Learning basic German helps with daily life, and we offer language coaching."
  - q: "How long does the whole process take?"
    a: "Start 12 to 18 months before your intake. University application takes 2 to 4 months, visa processing 6 to 12 weeks after your admission letter."
  - q: "Can I work while studying?"
    a: "Yes. Up to 120 full days per year at €12–€15 per hour. After graduation you get an 18-month job seeker visa to find full-time work."
  - q: "What happens after graduation, can I stay?"
    a: "Yes. Germany gives an 18-month Job Seeker Visa after graduation. Get a job, convert to a work permit, and apply for Permanent Residency after a few years."
  - q: "What is the APS certificate?"
    a: "A mandatory document verification by the German embassy that confirms your Indian qualifications meet German standards. Takes 4 to 8 weeks. We guide you through the full application."
  - q: "Can a student with a study gap apply?"
    a: "Yes. German universities are flexible about gaps if you explain them clearly in your SOP. A gap of 1 to 3 years is generally acceptable with a strong statement."
