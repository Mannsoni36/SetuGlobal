  - q: "What documents are required for a Germany student visa?"
    a: "Key documents include: university admission letter, valid passport, proof of financial resources (blocked account of €11,208+), health insurance, academic certificates with certified translations, APS certificate, biometric photos, and a filled visa application form. We prepare and review every document with you."
  - q: "What is a blocked account (Sperrkonto) and how much do I need?"
    a: "A blocked account (Sperrkonto) is a mandatory bank account required by the German embassy to prove you can fund your stay. As of 2024, you need to deposit €11,208 annually. The funds are released monthly (€934/month) once you are in Germany. We guide you through opening it with recommended providers like Fintiba or Expatrio."
  - q: "Do I need to know German to study in Germany?"
    a: "No — not to start! Hundreds of master's programs at German universities are fully taught in English. For English-taught programs, you need IELTS (6.0–6.5) or TOEFL. However, learning basic German helps with daily life, and we can recommend language courses."
  - q: "How long does the entire Germany application + visa process take?"
    a: "Ideally 12–18 months before your target intake. The university application itself takes 2–4 months. Visa processing takes 6–12 weeks after the admission letter. We strongly recommend starting early — especially for Winter Semester (October) and Summer Semester (April) intakes."
  - q: "What is the cost of studying and living in Germany?"
    a: "Public universities charge €0–€350/semester as administrative fees only — no tuition fees. Private universities range from €5,000–€20,000/year. Living costs vary by city: Munich is higher at €1,200–€1,500/month while cities like Leipzig or Dresden are very affordable at €700–€900/month."
  - q: "Can I work while studying in Germany?"
    a: "Yes! International students in Germany can legally work up to 120 full days (or 240 half days) per year. Part-time jobs typically pay €12–€15/hour, which can cover a significant portion of your monthly living expenses."
  - q: "What happens after graduation — can I stay in Germany?"
    a: "Absolutely. Germany offers an 18-month Job Seeker Visa after graduation, allowing you to stay and find employment. Once you have a job offer, you can convert it into a work permit. After 5 years of working in Germany, you can apply for Permanent Residency (PR)."
